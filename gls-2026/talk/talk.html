Imprecision

  • Imprecise (uses of) expressions: a little like vague expressions, e.g., tall.
    1. The Empire State Building is tall.
    • tall doesn’t let us know exactly how tall the ESB is.
    • Sort of like dry doesn’t always let us know exactly how dry someone is.

Sorites

  • But imprecision is different.
    • Sorites paradox:
        1. The ESB is tall.
        2. A building 1 mm shorter than a tall building is tall.
        3. Therefore, a one-story house is tall.
    • Compare:
        1. A theater with no people in it is empty.
        2. A theater with one more person in it than an empty theater is empty.

Pragmatic halos

Lasersohn proposes to understand imprecision in terms of a pragmatic halo (Lasersohn (1999)).

  • ⟦five⟧ = {4:55, 4:56, …, 5:00, … 5:04, 5:05}
  • ⟦X is dry⟧ = {True if 0 droplets, True if at most 1 droplet, …,
                            True if at most 20 droplets}
  • ⟦X is empty⟧ = {True if 0 seats filled, True if at most 1 seat
                                   filled, …}

Shrinking/widening the halo

Slack regulators shrink or widen the halo.

  1. Widening: Wow, it’s already about five!

  2. Shrinking: Wow, it’s already exactly five!

  3. Shrinking: The theater is completely empty.

Changing the standard of precision

The standard of precision (SOP) can change (Lewis (1979)).

  1. Jo: It’s already five.     

    Bo: No, it’s 4:58!

  2. Jo: The theater is empty.

    Bo: No, someone’s sitting in the corner!

  3. Jo: Okay, I’m dry.

    Bo: No, you have a few drops on your arm.

Unidirectionality

Compare:

  1. Jo: It’s already five. Bo: No, it’s 4:58!

    Jo: # Okay, but it’s still five!

  2. Jo: The theater is empty. Bo: No, someone’s in the corner.

    Jo: # Sure, but it’s empty.

  3. Jo: Okay, I’m dry. Bo: No, you have a few drops on your arm.

    Bo: # Sure, but I’m dry.

↝ Only possible to shrink the pragmatic halo?

Summary

  • A precise bot raises the SOP.
  • But an imprecise bot can lower it (a little)!
  • We have:
    • bidirectionality
    • bias toward raising the SOP/shrinking the pragmatic halo

Modeling pragmatic halos

Hypothesis: biased bidirectionality arises from our probabilistic knowledge about pragmatic halos.

  • Higher standards of precision are more likely;
  • but lower standard of precision are still possible.

Detour: scale structure

  • Maximum standard absolute adjectives: empty, full, clean
    • these can be used imprecisely
  • Relative adjectives: tall, wide, long
    • these are vague

This difference is reflected in scale structure.

Two models

Absolute model: there is an upper endpoint on the scale, and we model the SOP as departing from this endpoint.

Two models

Relative model: there is no upper endpoint - the adjectival standard is vague.

Schematically…

  • We design Bayesian hierarchical mixed effects models.
  • Ordered logistic likelihoods
  • Acceptance of the adjective and judgments about the dialogue are predicted using:
    • parameters sensitive to adjectival scale structure;
    • probabilities computed in terms of these parameters.
  • We fit both models to Block 1 only, as well jointly to all three blocks.

Model comparisons

Summary and future

We have some preliminary evidence that the apparent unidirectionality of precisification arises from:

  • lexical meaning (adjectival scale structure), combined with
  • prior probabilistic knowledge about pragmatic halos.

Ongoing:

  • We need similar data involving vague (rather than imprecise) adjectives. This would:
  • test the prediction that the relative model would perform better than the absolute model in this case.
